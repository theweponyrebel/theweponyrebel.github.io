About this Section

This Section will focus on Pacific Northwest fishing, In this section we will focus on two places in the Pacific Northwest Seattle and Alaska. This will teach you saltwater and freshwater fishing in both of these places.

Types of Fish to fish here

Saltwater

1. King salmon, also known as chinook salmon. Weight ranges from 10-100 lbs. Most sought after salmon due to size and strength. Typically fished for by trolling in a boat utilizing a downrigger, flasher, and herring (either full herring or cutplug).

2. Coho salmon, also known as silver salmon. Weight ranges from 5-25 lbs. Second most sought after due to size and strength. Typically fished for by trolling in a boat utilizing a downrigger, flasher, and herring (either full herring or cutplug).

3. Pink salmon, also known as humpys due to hump that grows on back of males during spawning season. Weight ranges from 3-8 lbs. Not typically targeted by sport fisherman, but can caught when fishing for kings or cohos.

4. Chum salmon, also known as dog salmon as the fish can is not good to eat and would preferably be fed to a dog. Weight ranges from 6-15 lbs. Not typically targeted by sport fisherman, but can caught when fishing for kings or cohos.

5. Sockeye salmon, also known as red salmon due to color of meat. Weight ranges from 5-14 lbs. Hard to catch in saltwater as their main diet is plankton, unlike the other four species.
